## The North Star

Working professionals aged 25–45 are quietly terrified. Not loudly. They're not posting "AI will take my job" — they're Googling it at 11pm. They're watching LinkedIn fill with AI hype they don't understand. They feel the ground shifting under a career they built over a decade.

Still Relevant exists to be the calm, knowing voice in that room. The account that says: *you're not behind. Here's the actual playbook.*

**The emotion we're targeting:** Not panic. Not inspiration porn. The quiet relief of someone finally getting a straight answer.

---

## The Snowball Framework

### Phase 1: Viral Seeds (Weeks 1–4)
**Goal:** Get found. Build the premise.

Content is provocative but true. Hook-heavy. Each video plants a flag: *Still Relevant is the account that tells you what everyone else is dancing around.*

The algorithm rewards watch-through rate. Faceless text-on-screen content with tight pacing and a clear payoff gets watched all the way through — especially when it's information the viewer needs.

Metrics to hit before moving to Phase 2:
- 3+ videos with 50K+ views
- 500+ followers
- Comment section showing the target anxiety ("this is exactly me", "saving this", "how have I never heard of this")

### Phase 2: Community Formation (Weeks 5–10)
**Goal:** Build belonging. Make them feel seen.

Content shifts from "let me tell you something" to "you already know this, let's go deeper." Reply videos (to their comments, text-only), "what you all told me" recaps, "you asked about X" videos.

This is when the comment section becomes the product. Pin the best comments. Respond in video format. Create a feedback loop where your community feels like they're building something together.

Metrics to hit before moving to Phase 3:
- Regular comments from returning viewers (same handles appearing)
- Saves-to-views ratio above 8% (saves = high intent = they'll buy)
- DM volume picking up
- "When does this come out?" starting to appear

### Phase 3: Conversion (Weeks 11+)
**Goal:** Turn community into customers.

At this point you don't have to sell hard. The community is warm. Introduce the offer naturally, as the thing they've been waiting for. Use scarcity (waitlist closes), social proof (comment reactions), and urgency (price goes up at launch).

Content during this phase: case studies (real or hypothetical), "here's what's in it" reveals, countdown content, "you asked if this was for you" videos.

## Content Pillars

Four rotating themes — each one speaks to a different facet of the core fear.

### Pillar 1: The Reality Check
**Theme:** Cutting through AI hype to say what's actually true.
**Tone:** Calm, authoritative, slightly contrarian.
**Format:** Text on dark/cream background. No frills.
**The promise:** "I'll tell you what the tech bros and the doom-scrollers won't."
**Example topic:** "The jobs AI is actually replacing (and it's not what they tell you)"

### Pillar 2: The Skill Inventory
**Theme:** The human skills that are becoming *more* valuable, not less.
**Tone:** Quietly optimistic. Evidence-based.
**Format:** Slideshow/list reveals. Text cards.
**The promise:** "You have more than you think. Here's what to build on."
**Example topic:** "5 skills AI literally cannot replicate (and you probably have them)"

### Pillar 3: The Playbook
**Theme:** Specific, actionable tactics — prompts, frameworks, workflows.
**Tone:** Tactical, efficient. Respects the viewer's intelligence.
**Format:** Screen recording, animated text, step-by-step reveals.
**The promise:** "Here's the actual tool, not just the concept."
**Example topic:** "The 3-prompt workflow that saves me 4 hours every week"

### Pillar 4: The Mirror
**Theme:** Naming the feelings — FOBO, impostor syndrome, AI anxiety — without therapy-speak.
**Tone:** Wry, knowing, a little dry humor. Human.
**Format:** Text-on-screen with relatable framing. POV format.
**The promise:** "You're not alone. This is the thing nobody's saying out loud."
**Example topic:** "POV: you googled 'will AI replace [your job]' at midnight again"

## Hook Formula Library

Hooks for this audience must do three things in 2 seconds:
1. **Trigger recognition** — "that's me"
2. **Promise a payoff** — "they're going to tell me something"
3. **Create mild dread or urgency** — "I need to watch this"

### Hook Templates

**The Naming Hook** (Pillar 4)
> "Nobody talks about [specific fear]. Let me."
> "There's a word for what you're feeling and it's not just anxiety."

**The Contradiction Hook** (Pillar 1)
> "Everything you've been told about AI at work is wrong."
> "The jobs AI is replacing first aren't the ones you think."
> "Hot take: your company's AI strategy is going to hurt you specifically."

**The List Reveal Hook** (Pillar 2)
> "5 skills that become MORE valuable when AI takes over. Save this."
> "The one thing on your résumé that AI cannot compete with."

**The Tactical Hook** (Pillar 3)
> "The 3-prompt workflow that does [X task] in 8 minutes instead of 4 hours."
> "I built a system for [common work task] with AI. Here's the exact template."
> "Stop using ChatGPT like this. Start using it like this."

**The POV Hook** (Pillar 4)
> "POV: everyone at your company is suddenly 'AI-powered' and you feel invisible."
> "POV: you've been good at your job for 10 years and suddenly it doesn't feel like enough."

## TikTok → Waitlist → Sale: The Full Funnel

### Layer 1: TikTok (Awareness + Trust)
Every video ends with the same frictionless CTA: "Follow for the playbook."
No hard sell. No link in bio push. Just follow + keep watching.
Bio: "Human guide to staying relevant when AI changes everything. Waitlist open ↓"

### Layer 2: The Waitlist (Email Capture)
Link in bio → stillrelevanthq.com (the waitlist page)
**Conversion catalyst:** After a video does 50K+ views, post a follow-up: "A lot of you asked about the guide. Waitlist is open — link in bio." 
The move: let the comments create the demand signal, then meet it.

### Layer 3: Email Nurture (Pre-sell)
Once on the waitlist, 3-email sequence:
1. **Welcome:** "You're on the list. Here's why I built this." (story email, builds relationship)
2. **Value drop:** Deliver one immediately-useful thing (a framework, a prompt template) — proof the guide is worth buying
3. **The offer:** Launch email. Waitlist price. Time-limited.

### Layer 4: Sale
Waitlist-only early access at reduced price. Create genuine scarcity (not fake countdown timers — actual tiered pricing: first 100, next 500, etc.)

## Engagement Tactics (Faceless, No-Camera)

### Reply With Video (Text-Only)
When a comment is gold, make a reply video — but text-only. "Someone asked: [quote]. Here's the actual answer:" Then deliver it in text-on-screen format. This trains the algorithm AND rewards your community.

### Pin Comments Strategically
Pin a comment that does the selling for you: "this account is the only one that actually explains this without hype — been here since day one." Community voice > brand voice.

### POV Comment Bait
End videos with an open question in text: "Tell me: what's the one AI thing your company says you need to understand but nobody actually explained?" 
The comments become content fuel and community research.

### Saves Campaign
Create one "save this" video per week — a resource, a list, a framework — explicitly designed to be bookmarked. High save rate trains the algorithm to show your content to people like your savers. This is how you reach more of the right audience without paid ads.

### Series Hooks
Use series titles that create returning viewers: "The AI-Proofing Playbook (Part 3 of 6)." Viewers who watch Part 3 go back for Parts 1 and 2. Watch time goes up. Algorithm notices.

### Comment Back in the First Hour
For the first hour after posting: respond to every comment, even with a single question. Engagement velocity in the first 60 minutes is the biggest algorithmic signal. Keep it text-based, keep it warm, keep it substantive.
